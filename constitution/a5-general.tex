	\protect\section{Resignation and Impeachment\add[S]{ procedure}}
	In the event of an \add[S]{elected }USG Member resigning, not being able, not being willing to continue their tasks as a Member, or being successfully impeached, the \hyperref[USGParliamentDef]{USG Parliament} will appoint an interim for the remaining period of the resigning member.\add[S]{ For resignations and impeachments the following rules apply: }
	
	\protect\begin{parenum}
		\item Any elected member of the USG may resign provided that the USG is informed two (2) weeks before the intended date of resignation.		
		\item Any member of the USG shall be impeached by either of the two: \change[S]{Two thirds (2/3) of the (other) elected members or referendum. }{An absolute 2/3 majority vote within the Parliament or a referendum. }Such a vote or referendum must clearly state the grounds for impeachment. Repeated \add[S]{unexcused }absence \add[P]{of three (3) }\remove[RS]{consecutive}\add[P]{ meetings or more} and/or not accomplishing designated tasks automatically start the process of impeachment of a \add[S]{Committee or Parliament }member:
		\begin{enumerate}
			\item The accused member and the impeacher (if any) have one (1) week time to \remove[RS]{argument and }present their case.
			\item At the next \hyperref[USGParliamentDef]{USG Parliament} meeting, the impeachment is voted upon.
		\end{enumerate}
		
		\item After resignation or impeachment, an officer may not run again for the same office within the term that the resignation \add[P]{or impeachment took place}.
	\end{parenum}

	\protect\section{Conflicts of Interest}
	Conflicts of interest must be declared and the involved members must be omitted from voting. In case of a tie in the voting procedure, the final decision is made jointly by the President, Vice-President and the respective Committee Head.
